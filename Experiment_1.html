1Ô∏è‚É£ Fundamentals of Linear Algebra and Statistics AS102

L-T-P: 3-1-0 | Credits: 4

Course Content

  1. Unit I ‚Äì Vector Spaces: Vector Spaces, subspace, elementary properties of vector spaces, sum of subspaces, linear combination, linear span, linear dependence and independence, basis, dimension and range.
  2. Unit II ‚Äì Linear Transformation: Linear transformation, Rank, nullity, kernel and image of linear transformation. Rank-nullity theorem, matrix of linear transformation, change of basis, Inverse of a linear transformation, matrix associated with a linear map and its application in Engineering.
  3. Unit III ‚Äì Matrices and its Applications: Types of Matrices, Inverse of a matrix using Gauss Jordan Method, Rank of a matrix, Echelon form, Normal form of a Matrix, System of linear homogeneous and non-homogeneous equations, characteristic equation of a matrix, Eigen values, Eigenvectors and Cayley Hamilton theorem (without proof), Application of Matrices.
  4. Unit IV ‚Äì Statistical Techniques -I: Introduction, Collection and Classification of data, Graphical Representation, Measures of central tendency, Measures of dispersions (Range, Quartile deviation, Mean deviation, Standard deviation), Coefficient of variation, Standard deviations of combination of two groups, Moments, Skewness, Kurtosis.
  5. Unit V ‚Äì Statistical Techniques -II: Curve Fitting, Method of least squares, fitting of straight lines, Fitting of second-degree parabola, Exponential curves, Correlation and Karl Pearson's coefficients of correlation, Rank correlation, Regression (linear and non-linear), Multiple Linear Regression and their applications in engineering analysis, prediction, and decision-making.

Course Outcomes (COs)

  1. Apply concepts of linear algebra and statistics in engineering contexts.
  2. Analyze vector spaces and linear transformations.
  3. Compute eigenvalues and eigenvectors for matrices.
  4. Use matrix algebra to solve engineering problems.

Textbooks: Singh and Pundir - Linear Algebra and Matrices, A Pragati Edition ; David C. Lay, Steven R. Lay, Linear Algebra and its applications, Pearson ; S. P. Gupta, Statistical Methods, Sultan Chand & Sons 46th edition

2Ô∏è‚É£ Applied Physics AS101

L-T-P: 3-1-0 | Credits: 4

Course Content

  1. Unit I ‚Äì Quantum Physics: Need of Quantum mechanics, Wave-Particle Duality. Group and Phase velocity, wave function and its interpretation, Schrodinger equation (1D), Particle in a box (infinite potential well), Introduction to Quantum tunnelling, Quantum computing and cryptography.
  2. Unit II ‚Äì Semiconductor & devices: Classification of solids on the basis of energy band gap, types of semiconductor, Concept of Fermi level, position of fermi level in intrinsic and extrinsic semiconductors, mobility, effect of temperature semiconductor,on conductivity of Carrier generation/recombination phenomena, Formation of PN junction, Depletion region, built-in potential, Working of solar cell, Photo diode and LEDs.
  3. Unit III ‚Äì Engineering Optics: Laser: Basic of Laser and characteristics, Einstein's coefficient, Ruby laser, He-Ne laser and semiconductor laser, Applications of Laser- LIDAR, holography and optical information security. Optical fibre: Structure of an Optical fibre; Acceptance angle, Numerical aperture, V- Number, Classification of optical fibre, fibre Losses, Advantages and applications of optical fibre- Data transmission, medical imaging, fibre optic communication system
  4. Unit IV ‚Äì Electromagnetic Theory: Physical significance of gradient, divergence and curl, Concept of Displacement current, Maxwell's equations, Poynting Vector & theorem, EM waves and its propagation in free space and conducting medium, skin depth, Applications of EM waves Communication (qualitative)
  5. Unit V ‚Äì Emerging Physics for Engineering Applications: Basics of Nanophysics, Quantum confinement, Applications in nanotechnology. 1D, 2D, 3D Nanomaterials, Fabrication techniques of nanomaterials: - Chemical Vapor Deposition, Sol-Gel, Applications of Nanotechnology- Quantum Dot, nanoscale memory, air and water purification, cosmetic industry. Superconductors: Meissner effect, temperature dependence of critical field, Type I & Type II superconductors. Introduction to Spintronics, Giant Magnetoresistance (GMR) for memory storage.

Course Outcomes

  • Understand the basics of quantum mechanics and its applications.
  • Analyze the properties and applications of semiconductors and optical devices.
  • Apply electromagnetic theory to engineering problems.
  • Apply knowledge of lasers, fiber optics and modern materials.

Textbooks: S. L. Gupta, Physics Vol. 1 & 2, Shubham Publications ; S. K. Gupta, Engineering Physics- I & II, Krishna Prakashan ; S. Prakash, A. Kumar, S.L. Rajput, C. Garg, Engineering Physics Vol I & II ; Navneet Gupta. Engineering Phvsics bv Navneet Gupta. Dhanpat

3Ô∏è‚É£ Basics of Electrical Engineering EL101

L-T-P: 3-1-0 | Credits: 4

Course Content

  1. Unit I - Fundamentals and Analysis of DC Circuits:
    - Introduction to electrical networks and classification of circuit elements: active and passive, linear and nonlinear, unilateral and bilateral, lumped and distributed.
    -Types of sources and source transformation techniques.
    -Application of Kirchhoff‚Äôs laws (KVL and KCL).
    -Mesh analysis and nodal analysis.
    -Network theorems: Superposition theorem, Thevenin‚Äôs theorem and Norton‚Äôs theorem (with independent sources only).
  2. Unit II - Steady-State Analysis of AC Circuits:
    -Single-phase circuits: Analysis of single-phase R, L, C, RL, RC, and RLC series circuits, Complex notation-based analysis of parallel RLC circuits.
    -Active, reactive, and apparent power.
    -Resonance in AC circuits: Series and Parallel resonance and their resonance curves.
    -Three-phase circuits: voltage and current relations in balanced star and delta connections.
  3. Unit III - Single Phase Transformer:
    -Magnetic circuits: Analogy between electric and magnetic circuits, Faraday‚Äôs law of electromagnetic induction, B-H loop and hysteresis losses.
    -Transformers: working principle, EMF equation, ideal and practical transformers.
    -Equivalent circuit, losses, efficiency, and numerical problems on efficiency.
  4. Unit IV - Electrical Machines:
    -DC Machines: Construction, working principle, types, EMF equation and numerical related to EMF equation, torque equation of motor and applications.
    -Three-phase Induction Motors: Operating principle, concept of slip and numerical problems related to slip, applications of three phase induction motor.
    -Single-phase Induction Motors: working principle and applications.
  5. Unit V - Power System and Recent Trends:
    -Overview of the electrical power system with single-line diagram.
    -Introduction to grid systems, micro-grids, and smart grids.
    -Distributed Generation (DG): Types, benefits, and challenges.
    -Need and process of Earthing.
    -Batteries: Classification, efficiency and backup calculations.
    -Battery Management System (BMS): Block diagram and advantages.
    -Battery Energy Storage System (BESS): Need and advantages in modern electrical networks.
    -Role and applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in modern power systems.

COs

  • Analyze electrical circuits using network laws.
  • Explain operation of basic electrical machines.
  • Understand principles of transformers and power systems.
  • Apply electrical engineering concepts to real-world problems.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Data Structures CS101

L-T-P: 3-0-2 | Credits: 4

Units

  1. Unit I - Introduction to Programming:
    Introduction to components of a computer system: Memory, processor, I/O Devices, storage, operating system, Concept of assembler, compiler, interpreter, loader and linker.
    Idea of Algorithm: Representation of Algorithm, Flowchart, Pseudo code with examples, From algorithms to programs, source code.

    Iteration and loops: use of while, do while and for loops, multiple loop variables, use of break and continue statements.
    Functions: Introduction, types of functions, functions with array, passing parameters to functions, call by value, call by reference, recursive functions.
  4. Unit IV - Arrays & Basic Algorithms: Pointer arithmetic, string handling functions.
  5. Unit V - Pointers & File Handling: Structure definition, file operations, practical implementation.

COs

  • To develop simple algorithms for arithmetic and logical problems.

  • To implement conditional branching, iteration and recursion. .

Course Content
